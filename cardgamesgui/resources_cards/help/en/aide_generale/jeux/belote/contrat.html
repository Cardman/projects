Bids fix the deal.

Each player speaks, in counter clock wise,
by beginning by the player at the right of the dealer.

A player has to either pass or bid.

At the classic belote, one processes by two rounds:

  1. At the first round:
     If a player bids, then one''s takes the "top" card and bidding is stopped in order to go to play cards.
  2. At the second round:
     If a player bids, then one''s declares a suit which is going to lead the other one during this deal.
     In this case, one''s cannot declare the suit of the "top" card.
     One''s takes the "top" card and bidding is stopped in order to go to play cards.

If someone takes, then the dealer deals two cards to the taker and three cards to the other players by beginning by the starter (the player who begins).

Meaning: the second round is played only if everybody has pass.

At the coinche, one processes by the following way:

  1. A player can pass or declare a suit with a number of points to score.
     The declared points vary by ten to ten from 80 to 160.
     Declaring 162 points means the player must make all tricks and begins playing
  2. For over bidding, il faut annoncer une couleur avec dix points de plus.
  3. Even by passing while a round, bidding a suit with points is possible while a latter round.
  4. If three consecutive players pass, then bidding is stopped in order to go to play cards.

Meaning: if a player declares 162 points for a suit, then bidding is stopped, as other players must pass.

The taker plays with the player on the opposite side against the two other players.
