Belote is played card by card.
A trick is a set of cards won by a player having played the strongest card. The player having won a trick begins the next trick.(Example: the player having played the Jack of the trump suit begins the next trick.).

The player who starts a trick is a starter.
The player who has played the strongest card of a trick is the winner of trick, this player wins the trick.

The demanded suit is the suit of the first played card of a trick.
A trick is trumped if there is at lest one trump.

  1. The first card of a trick
    The player at the right of the dealer begins to play. This player can play any card.
  2. Rules
    1. If the dominant suit is the trump suit,
      1. If the player does not own any trump card,
        one can play any card.
      2. Else If this last one can overtrump (play a greater trump),
        then one must overtrump,
      3. else, one must follow a trump card,
      4. else one can play any card.
    2. If the dominant suit is not the trump suit,
      1. If the player owns cards of the dominant suit,
        then this one must follow the suit without playing a stronger card.
      2. Else, if one''s partner leads the trick,
        then one can play any card.
      3. Else if the player does not own any trump card, one can play any card.
      4. Else If the trick is not trumped,
        then one must use a trump card of one''s choice, (the player trumps).
      5. Else if the player can play a trump card stronger than the maximal played trump card,
        then one must use a trump card that one can play, (the player overtrumps).
      6. Else one can play any card.
