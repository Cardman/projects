Bids determine the deal.

Each player declares a bid, in the counter clock wise,
by beginning by the player at the right of the dealer.

A player must either declare "pass", or declare a bid greater than the previous bids.
Each player has to declare once.

At tarot, there are 5 bids, in the order from the lowest to the greatest:

  1. Pass
  2. Take
  3. Guard
  4. Guard without
  5. Guard against

After that all players have bid, the player having declared the strongest bid becomes the taker.
In function of the declared bid by the taker, about the dog, one processes by the following way:
  1. Take: the taker takes the dog and shows it to the other players.
  2. Guard: the taker takes the dog and shows it to the other players.
  3. Guard without: the taker takes the dog but the dog stays hidden.
  4. Guard against: the taker leaves the dog to the opponents of the player and the dog stays hidden.


Meanings:

  1. The opponents of the taker are the defense.
  2. There is only one round of bidding.(Each player declares only once.)

Examples:

  1. If the 1st player, who must speak, declares "pass",
      if the 2nd declares "Take",
      then the 3rd cannot declare "Take", but the 3rd can declare "Guard" or greater.
  2. If the 1st player, who must speak, declares "pass",
      if the 2nd declares "Take",
      if the 3rd declares "pass",
      if the last one declares "Guard without",
      then the taker is the last player.
  3. If the 1st player, who must speak, declares "pass",
      if the 2nd declares "Guard against",
      then, the two other players must declares "pass".

The taker plays against the three other players.
