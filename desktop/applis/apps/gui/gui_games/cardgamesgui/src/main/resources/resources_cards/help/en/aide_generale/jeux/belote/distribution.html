The player who deals cards is called dealer.
From a deal to an other, the dealer changes,
that is say the next dealer is the player at the right of the previous dealer,
and the cards are mixed and cut at each time.

At the classic belote,
each player owns, at the beginning, 5 cards. The remaining cards (equals to 12) make up a deck,
the cards are dealt 3 by 3, then 2 by 2.

Once all players have five cards, the "top" card of the deck must be shown.

At the coinche,
each player owns 8 cards,
the cards are dealt 3 by 3, then 2 by 2 for the last one.

The dealer deals in the counter clock wise.
