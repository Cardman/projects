Here is the differences between bids:

  1. Bidding at classic belote
    1. At the first round, a player can bid one of the following bids:
      1. Suit
           In this case, the trump suit will exist and will be the suit of the "top" card.
      2. No trump
           In this case, the order of cards, for all suits, is Ace-10-King-Queen-Jack-9-8-7.
      3. All trump
           In this case, the order of cards, for all suits, is Jack-9-Ace-10-King-Queen-8-7.
    2. At the second round, a player can bid one of the following bids:
      1. Other suit
           In this case, the trump suit will exist and will be the suit declared by the player.
           In this same case, the player cannot declare the suit of the "top" card.
      2. No trump
           In this case, the order of cards, for all suits, is Ace-10-King-Queen-Jack-9-8-7.
      3. All trump
           In this case, the order of cards, for all suits, is Jack-9-Ace-10-King-Queen-8-7.
  2. Bidding at coinche
    1. Suit
         In this case, the trump suit will exist and will be the last declared suit.
    2. No trump
         In this case, the order of cards, for all suits, is Ace-10-King-Queen-Jack-9-8-7.
    3. All trump
         In this case, the order of cards, for all suits, is Jack-9-Ace-10-King-Queen-8-7.
    4. Bids No trump and All trump are declared like suits.

     Belote rebelote is not allowed for bids "no trump" and "all trump".
  3. Play cards
    1. If the declared bid is "no trump":

      1. If the player owns cards of the dominant suit,
        then the player must follow a card of the the dominant suit without playing a greater card.
      2. Else the player can play any card.
    2. If the declared bid is "all trump":

      1. If the player does not own any card of the dominant suit,
        then the player can play any card.
      2. Else, if the player can play a greater trump,
        then the player must play a greater trump.
      3. Else, the player follows a trump card.
  4. End of deal
      Value of cards:
    1. If the declared bid is "no trump":
      • Ace: 19 points
      • 10: 10 points
      • King: 4 points
      • Queen: 3 points
      • Jack: 2 points
      • 9, 8 et 7: 0 point
    2. If the declared bid is "all trump":
      • Jack: 14 points
      • 9: 9 points
      • Ace: 6 points
      • 10: 4 points
      • King: 3 points
      • Queen: 2 points
      • 8 et 7: 0 point

 The calculation of scores while a bid is the same as the classic belote.
