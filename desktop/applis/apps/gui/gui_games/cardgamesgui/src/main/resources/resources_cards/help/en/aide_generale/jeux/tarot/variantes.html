As all card games, there are variants.

Here is some variants:

  1. The declaring miseres can be used:
    1. Misere of trump: no trump card(The Excuse is a trump card here).
    2. Misere of points: neither Oudler nor characters.
    3. Misere of low cards: no low card from ace to ten.
    4. Misere of suit: only trump cards and possibly the Excuse.
    5. Misere of characters: no characters card.
  2. The tarot can be played by 3 players.
      By convention the taker wins only if the taker scores with one more point than necessary,
      (If a half of point is missing for the taker, then the taker looses the deal.).
  3. With 4 players, other dealings are possible,
    1. with a call at a king or at a point card.
    2. without call, in this case, each player is the partner of the player in the opposite side.
  4. The tarot can be played by 5 players. One of the following dealings is possible:
    1. Call of a king.
    2. Call of a character.
    3. The taker plays against the 4 other players.
      By convention, the taker wins only if the taker scores with one more point than necessary,
      (If a half of point is missing for the taker, then the taker looses the deal.).
  5. The tarot can be played by 6 players. One of the following dealings is possible:
    1. Call of a king.
    2. Call of a character.
    3. The taker is the partner of the player in the opposite side.
  6. While the number of players is odd, the rule of half point can be changed.
    It is possible:
    1. The end is classic (The taker looses the deal by one point by rounding.).
    2. The match is equality (Nobody scores point at last.).
    3. The taker wins the deal by one point by rounding.
  7. Slam can be regarded as a bid greater than "Guard against".
      In this case, it is impossible to declare it as additional declaring.
      In this case, the dog is hidden and belongs to the defense.
  8. Playing discards is to loose the most cards as possible, in the following cases:
    1. If there is no bidding.
    2. If all players declare "pass".

      In the two cases, there is no team.
  9. Playing one for one is to win the most cards as possible, in the following cases:
    1. If there is no bidding.
    2. If all players declare "pass".

      In the two cases, there is no team.
  10. Certain declarings may be allowed.
  11. Cards can be dealt clock wise.
  12. Cards may not be mixed.
  13. Cards may be mixed once.
Variants numbered from 1 to 5 and 8 and 9 will be seen in detail.
